To assess the effectiveness of our method for RCA, we compare it
to the following five baselines. Dummy takes the list of all metrics,
randomly shuffles it, and returns the result. Following previous
work [31], we use this method to evaluate against random selection.
CausalRCA [46] utilizes a DAG-GNN [48] to build a weighted met-
ric causality graph. Subsequently, CausalRCA employs the PageR-
ank [7] algorithm to generate a ranked list of potential root causes.
CIRCA [18] builds a structural graph based on system architec-
ture information and causal assumptions, identifying root causes
through regression-based hypothesis testing that detects metrics
with significant deviations from their expected distributions. As in
previous work [31], due to the lack of system architecture infor-
mation, we employ the PC algorithm [37] to construct the graph
instead. N-Sigma [18, 19] applies a z-scoring to each metric individ-
ually by calculating the mean and standard deviation of the normal
period. Each data point in the abnormal period is evaluated based
on its deviation from the mean relative to the standard deviation.
The maximum deviation is used to rank all metrics. BARO [31] ap-
plies a z-scoring similar to N-Sigma. In contrast, it uses the median
